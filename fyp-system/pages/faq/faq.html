Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

What is FYP?

Final Year Project (FYP) is a mandatory course for all final year students. It involves developing a significant project that demonstrates the skills and knowledge acquired throughout your degree program.

How long is the FYP duration?

The FYP spans two semesters, typically starting in your final year. Each semester has different milestones and deliverables.

Project Requirements

What type of projects are acceptable?

Projects should be related to your field of study and demonstrate significant technical complexity. They should solve real-world problems and involve substantial programming or development work.

Can I work in a group?

No, FYP must be completed individually to demonstrate your personal capabilities and project management skills.

Supervision

How are supervisors assigned?

Supervisors are assigned based on project topic, expertise match, and availability. Students can indicate preferences but final assignments are made by the department.

How often should I meet my supervisor?

Regular meetings should be scheduled at least once every two weeks. Additional meetings can be arranged based on project needs and supervisor availability.

Assessment

How is the FYP evaluated?

Assessment includes project proposal (20%), progress presentations (30%), final report (30%), and project demonstration (20%). Specific criteria are provided in the project guidelines.

What happens if I fail?

If you fail, you will need to repeat the FYP in the following semester. This may delay your graduation. Ensure you maintain regular communication with your supervisor to stay on track.
